It was only a few hours ago that the «SS Île de France» left the port of New York with 940 passengers on board, bound for Europe. As is usual, a tugboat helped her leave the pier. Almost at the same time, the Stockholm left the port of New York. The Stockholm was a Swedish liner taking 534 passengers home to Gothenburg. It followed the «SS Île de France» through the Hudson River, but the «SS Île de France» was much faster, and the «SS Île de France» soon left it behind. Then heavy fog came up and it was now so thick that you couldn't see anything in front of your eyes. The «SS Île de France» therefore closed the watertight doors, positioned additional lookout posts, only drove on standby and constantly checked the radar.

The «SS Île de France»`s radar showed two signals, which were getting closer and closer to each other at a distance of 40 nautical miles behind the «SS Île de France» and then merging into one another.

The chief radio operator of the «SS Île de France» ran to the bridge and informed the captain that he had received SOS signals from the Andrea Doria. Everyone was confused. The Andrea Doria was an Italian luxury liner. Could it be that she was in distress?

Captain de Beaudéan had to react. He had a duty to arrive in France without delay. Should he turn back? If the emergency call wasn't justified, he would have to travel back to New York to refuel, many passengers and goods would be delayed, that would be the shipping company's responsibility cost a lot of money. But what if the emergency call was real and people's lives were in danger? He ordered the ship to turn around and approach the suspected accident site at maximum speed. The number of lookout posts was doubled again. The «SS Île de France» came to a stop about 400 meters from the Andrea Doria, which was unable to maneuver.
The fog revealed a gruesome sight. The Stockholm and the Andrea Doria had collided, and the reinforced bow of the Stockholm, which was designed for ice water, had cut open the starboard side of the Andrea Doria. The water had immediately entered and caused the Andrea Doria to list heavily.

The bow of the Stockholm was destroyed, but this liner managed to stay afloat for a few hours while the Andrea Doria slowly sank. Due to the heavy list, half of the lifeboats were not operational. It was a matter of life and death for 1,706 people.

Other ships had also heard the distress signal and turned to, but they were small and poorly equipped. The Stockholm hesitated to take on all of the Andrea Doria's passengers due to its massive damage.

Captain de Beaudéan had the «SS Île de France» maneuvered so that the damaged ships were as protected from the waves as possible. All lights were switched on on board the «SS Île de France» to show the people in distress that help was at hand. With the help of the small ships and the lifeboats, all but 46 passengers and crew members were eventually rescued.

The «SS Île de France» returned to New York immediately so that the injured survivors could receive professional care as quickly as possible. The crew was celebrated as heroes in New York Harbor.

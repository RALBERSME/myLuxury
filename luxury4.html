The new life of the «SS Île de France» after the year 1949:

It was only after Germany's capitulation that the «SS Île de France» returned to French hands. Years of repair work followed. After World War II, France no longer had any shiny ocean liners. The «SS Paris» burned down and sank on April 19, 1939, and the «SS Normandy» fell victim to the flames in 1942. As war reparations, France received the German luxury liner «SS Europa», which was the sister ship of the legendary «SS Bremen».

That is why the French put so much effort into converting the «SS Île de France» back into a stately liner. The three chimneys were removed and two larger, more modern chimneys were put in their place. The ship was repainted and given a modern interior. In 1949 it resumed its transatlantic services and once again impressed as an elegant luxury ship.
